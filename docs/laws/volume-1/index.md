**Period Covered:** 1799-1872  
**Historical Context:** Establishment of British colonial legal framework and foundational laws that shaped South Asian jurisprudence

## Overview

This foundational volume spans nearly three-quarters of a century, documenting the evolution from early East India Company regulations to comprehensive British colonial legislation. It represents the bedrock of South Asian legal system, containing landmark acts that continue to influence Bangladesh law today.

## Historical Significance

- **Colonial Foundation:** Earliest systematic legal framework in the subcontinent
- **Criminal Law Codification:** The Penal Code, 1860 - cornerstone of criminal law
- **Evidence System:** The Evidence Act, 1872 - foundation of judicial procedure
- **Social Reform:** Progressive legislation including Hindu Widow's Re-marriage Act
- **Administrative Framework:** Police, courts, and revenue administration establishment
